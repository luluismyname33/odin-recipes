Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a traditional Japanese dish that takes its English translation quite literally. "Oya" which means parent and "Ko" meaning child. It has chicken pieces cooked in a pan along with eggs to make a delicious combination. Served on top of rice and garnished with some green onion.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs - Recommend this over chicken breasts because the fattier thighs are more flavorful, tender, and forgiving (not like dry, overcooked breasts).
  • Sake - Drizzling a tablespoon of sake over chicken helps remove its gamey odor.
  • Onion
  • Eggs
  • Mitsuba - This Japanese wild parsley has a celery-like flavor with a slightly bitter taste. This garnish is used quite often in donburi (rice bowls) dishes.
  • Seasonings: Dashi (Japanese soup stock), soy sauce, mirin, and sugar.
  • Steamed rice
  • Shichimi Togarashi

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients: cut the onion, mitsuba/green onions, and chicken and crack the eggs.
  2. Cook the onion in the seasoning mixture.
  3. Add the chicken and cook until no longer pink.
  4. Drizzle the egg mixture at two seperate times. Garnish mitsuba/green onions.
  5. Spoon the finished dish over the bed of steamed rice in a donburi bowl.
